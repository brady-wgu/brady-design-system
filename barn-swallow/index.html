The Palette

Five Core Colors

Every shade drawn directly from barn swallow anatomy — from the iridescent dorsal cap to the russet throat patch.

#0A1128

Deep Steel Blue-Black

Primary text & dark backgrounds. Mirrors the iridescent dorsal plumage.

#FFCC00

Gape Yellow

Accent, CTAs on dark backgrounds. Named for the bright gape flanges of nestlings.

#D97443

Cinnamon / Tawny

AA-compliant surface color. The warm rufous tones of the breast and forehead.

#C2592A

Ember

Large text & UI components only (3.8:1). A deeper, richer cinnamon for emphasis.

#F7F4EF

Cream / Off-White

Default page background. Warmer and softer than pure white — evokes eggshell and nest down.

#1E2E56

Mid Steel Blue

Secondary dark surface — section headers, card accents. Bridges ink and cream.

Accessibility

WCAG 2.1 / 2.2 Compliance Levels

Every pairing is live — the text you see below is rendered at the actual contrast ratio described.

Level AAA  AAA · ≥7:1

Pair 1 — Ink on Cream

The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog. Readable at any size — comfortable for long-form body copy, captions, and footnotes.

Also works as Cream on Ink (same pair, reversed — see next card).

#0A1128 on #F7F4EF · Contrast ≈ 18:1 · AAA ✓

Level AAA  AAA · ≥7:1

Pair 2 — Gape Yellow on Ink

The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog. High-energy accent that still clears AAA — ideal for bold callouts and reversed hero panels.

Use sparingly; the high chroma is energetic by design.

#FFCC00 on #0A1128 · Contrast ≈ 9.5:1 · AAA ✓

Level AA  AA · ≥4.5:1

Pair 3 — Ink on Tawny

The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog. Passable for standard body text — meets the minimum required by WCAG 2.1 Success Criterion 1.4.3 for normal text.

#0A1128 on #D97443 · Contrast ≈ 5.2:1 · AA ✓

UI & Large Text Only  UI · ≥3:1

Large Heading in Ember

Body copy in ember at 0.85 rem — fails for normal text (3.8:1 < 4.5:1). Use only for 18pt+ headings, 14pt+ bold labels, or interactive UI chrome.

#C2592A on #F7F4EF · Contrast ≈ 3.8:1 · UI/Large text only ⚠

Tip: The AA Tawny pair improves significantly by lightening the background to a pale tan (e.g. #EDD5B8 → contrast ~8:1, AAA). The Ember color also achieves AA on white (#FFFFFF → ~4.6:1) if you need Ember in body text.
